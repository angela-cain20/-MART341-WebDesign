Halsey's
Debut Novel

Poet, Activist, Musician




Halsey is an American singer, songwriter, and actress who rose to fame in 2015 with her debut studio album "Badlands". Since then, she has become a prominent figure in the music industry, known for her unique vocal style, honest and personal lyrics, and her willingness to address social issues in her music.


Halsey's lyrics are deeply personal and often draw from her own life experiences. She has been open about her struggles with mental health issues, including bipolar disorder, and has addressed topics such as addiction, love, and heartbreak in her music. Her honesty and vulnerability have resonated with many listeners, and her music has become a source of comfort and inspiration for fans around the world.


In addition to her music, Halsey has also been a vocal advocate for social justice issues, using her platform to speak out about topics such as LGBTQ+ rights, racial inequality, and women's rights. She has been involved in various activism campaigns and has used her music to raise awareness about these issues.


Overall, Halsey is a talented artist who has made a significant impact in the music industry. Her unique sound, honest lyrics, and commitment to social justice have made her a role model for many young people, and she shows no signs of slowing down any time soon.

— Samples from Insider.





The spoken word poem 'Story of Mine', from Halsey's debut poetry book I Would Leave Me If I Could was performed by Halsey at the 2018 Women's March, detailing Halsey's experience with sexual assault. You can read an analysis of this performance here at Billboard. Story of Mine is a heart renching poem that talked of the horrors women and young girls have to face while merely existing in society. I Would Leave Me If I Could, has several other poems of the same vigor, showcasing the humanity behind a large pop star and her commitment to voicing wrongdoings in the name of societal change.
